The Problem

Sleep apnea is vastly underdiagnosed

Untreated sleep apnea raises the risk of heart disease, stroke, and diabetes — yet most people never receive a diagnosis because the path to one is too expensive and inaccessible.

🏥

Clinical studies are out of reach

A polysomnography (PSG) study requires an overnight hospital stay with 20+ sensors. Costs range from $1,500–$3,000 and wait times can stretch months.

😴

Consumer devices miss what matters

Smartwatches track SpO₂ and heart rate, but lack the acoustic data — snoring, wheezing, and apnea sounds — that are the clearest indicators of obstructive sleep apnea.

📋

No actionable reports for physicians

Even when patients record their own audio, there's no automated pipeline to classify events, compute AHI scores, or produce a clinical summary a doctor can act on.

🌍

Underserved communities bear the cost

Rural and low-income populations face the highest rates of undiagnosed apnea, the least access to sleep labs, and no affordable alternatives.

Our Solution

A wearable that listens while you sleep

Dr. Somno combines a thumb-sized embedded system, multi-sensor fusion, and intelligent signal processing into a device anyone can use at home.

1

Wear & Record

The device is worn on the chest during sleep. A PDM microphone records respiratory sounds at 16 kHz, an accelerometer tracks body movement, and a piezo sensor detects vibration — all saved to a microSD card.

2

Analyze

A Python signal processing pipeline segments the audio, extracts acoustic features, and runs a trained classifier to label every event: apnea, snoring, coughing, wheezing, movement, and normal breathing.

3

Report

The system generates an Apnea-Hypopnea Index (AHI) estimate, a full event timeline, and a plain-language summary — the same information a physician reviews after a home sleep test.
